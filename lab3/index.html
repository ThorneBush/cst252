The task we decided to go with is a mirror simulator,
where participants can place cubes on either side of a bar(mirror),
and it reflects the cubes an equal distance on the other side.

Task Outline

1. What actions can I take?

  • Placing Cubes
  • Moving the mirror up and down

2. What do I need to keep track of?

  • Keep track of the coordinates of the cube
  • Keep track of the coordinates of the bar
  • Keep track of the cubes placed

3. What do I need to do over and over again?

  • Check for new cubes placed
  • calculate coordinate positions

4. When do I have to make decisions?

  • Where on the y-axis the mirror is
  • Have to make a decision to subtract or
    add twice the distance between the cube and the mirror

Pseudocode Steps

Step 1

  • Placing Cubes
  • Moving the mirror up and down

Step 2

Check position of mirror on the y-axis

Step 3

Set cubes on screen, and check position
of the cube that is placed.
(Keep track of the x-axis of the cube.
It’s the same in the reflection)

Step 4

Check distance between the cube and the mirror

Step 5

If on top, subtract double the distance of
the cube’s y-axis from the y-axis of the mirror.
Add double if placed below

Step 6

Add that value to the y-axis of the reflection
(which should already have the x-axis from step 3)
